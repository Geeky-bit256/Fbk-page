Risk management

The first goal as a trader, is to protect your capital before thinking of making money.


Trading without undertanding money management is like driving a car with your eyes closed. The big mistake most beginner traders make is looking at the reward and now how much the are risking. This causes them to open large positions or many position not being cautious of how much they are risking because the truth is, no one that gets the markets 100% right all the time. You lose some days, so deciding how much are you giving to the makets if you lose is very important. The more you increase the lot size not fitting your account beacuse "If i win this trade I can make a lot of money" is the more you risk losing you entire account. You are literally telling the enemies, Im giving you this lot of money, and they will take it.
The question is that, will ypu account survive if you had 20 losses? 50 losses, 70 losses, would you still have money to trade? That is the question you should be asking never think that losing streak will never happen to you. Everyone losses. Its all part of the game. Protect the down side the upside will take care of itself.


The key is to make sure you risk no more than 1% of your capital. This is non negoatiable if you want to be profitable in the long run.

Emotions

Be the master of your emotions. This is a huge one because the reason why most traders lose is not because their strategy is bad, but because they let emotions rule.If you do not control them they will control you.


Let us start with the first one.

1. FEAR

Fear of losing money is caused my many things and one of them if deposting the money you cannot afford to lose. The act of depositing the money you cannot afford to lose comes from desperation.


This is when you trade because you need money to buy a new phone, or maybe you have the money for rent and now you want to double it. This is unacceptable in trading. Never trade because you are in need.


Deposit the money you can afford to lose always, always. Because the truth is: nothing is guaranteed, the market has no mercy, even professional traders lose. Depositing the money you are afraid to lose is just making things worse. Do not make it harder than it should. Please trader!


Say "Okay, so this is my R2000, even if i lose it, it wont affect my life. That way you remove that trading under pressure and the more you remove that, the clear you will make good trades done following the plan.

2. GREED

Greed causes many traders to want more leading to traders not taking profit or over leveraging.


The imagination of super cars and living your dream life after you deposited money, must come to an end.


It happens that a trader sees profit, fear says "Close now!", greed says "Hold, you making more money today", even when you see that it hit yout target level. Then later the market reverses, and you regret why you didnt close. Now the regret ruined your entire day.


Know when to take profits, the market can go to whatever direction, get and go out, this is not your home.


Have tp set it place. Do not rely on emotions for taking profit.

3. FOMO

This is fear of missing out. Many traders they get late into the market and they try to force the trade getting in because they dont want to miss out on that move.



If you come to the market late and you realise that the setup playout out, just leave it. Close the chart and continue with your day. Another setup will come. Dont treat trading like it is ending tomorrow. The market will always give you your setups, just wait. Patience.
